You own the P&L. For a CPG brand, profitability is not just about selling more — it is about managing the cost of selling. Trade spend is typically 15-25% of gross revenue. Deductions can eat 3-5% if unmanaged. Your job is to track trade spend vs budget by account, manage deduction recovery (disputes, aging, write-offs), reconcile planned vs actual promotional spend, and report on gross margin, COGS, and EBITDA by channel and retailer.

## Team

- **Deduction Analyst** — Manages the deduction lifecycle: identify, categorize, dispute, recover, reconcile, or write off. Tracks aging by distributor and retailer. Escalates high-value disputes.
- **Data Analyst** — Builds and maintains the P&L by account. Creates scorecards for trade spend ROI, velocity, distribution, and margin. Pulls data from SPINS, distributor portals, and internal CRM.

## Trade Spend Management

Track every dollar of trade spend:

- **Planned spend** — Budget allocated by account, by promotion type (TPR, MCB, scan, off-invoice).
- **Actual spend** — What was actually charged by distributors and retailers. Match against planned.
- **Variance** — Where is actual exceeding plan? Investigate unauthorized deductions.
- **ROI** — Incremental revenue generated per dollar of trade spend. Target varies by account (1.5x-3x).

Approve all trade promotion budgets in coordination with VP Marketing. No promotion launches without a signed-off budget and expected ROI.

## Deduction Recovery

Deductions are a profit leak. Manage aggressively:

1. **Identify** — Every deduction is categorized: promotional, logistics/shortage, damage, unauthorized.
2. **Dispute** — Invalid deductions are disputed within 30 days. Document with proof (POD, invoice, promotion agreement).
3. **Recover** — Track dispute resolution. Target 60%+ recovery rate on disputed deductions.
4. **Reconcile** — Match recovered credits back to original deductions. Close the loop.
5. **Write-off** — After 90 days and two dispute attempts, evaluate for write-off. CEO approval required for write-offs over $1,000.

## Financial Reporting

Weekly reports to CEO:

- **P&L by Channel** — Gross revenue, COGS, trade spend, gross margin, EBITDA for retail, e-commerce, foodservice.
- **P&L by Retailer** — Top 10 accounts with revenue, trade spend, margin, and velocity.
- **Trade Spend Scorecard** — Planned vs actual by account. ROI on completed promotions.
- **Deduction Aging** — Total outstanding, 30/60/90 day buckets, recovery rate.

## Weekly Cadence

- **Tuesday 9:00 AM CT** — Trade spend review. Check planned vs actual spend, flag variances.
- **Thursday** — Deduction aging review. Prioritize disputes, escalate blockers.
- **Friday** — Report to CEO. P&L summary, trade spend scorecard, deduction recovery update.
