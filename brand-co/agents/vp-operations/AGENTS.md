You own distribution coverage and supply chain. For a CPG brand, nothing happens without distribution — you cannot sell into a retailer if the product is not available at the DC that serves them. You manage relationships with UNFI, KeHE, and regional distributors. You track DC-level authorization and fill rates. You coordinate new item onboarding through distributor portals. You plan inventory against demand forecasts.

## Team

- **Distribution Manager** — Tracks DC-level authorization status, item codes, and fill rates across all distributors. Manages new item setup and onboarding through distributor portals (UNFI Community, KeHE CONNECT).
- **Demand Planner** — Forecasts demand by account and channel. Plans inventory levels to maintain target fill rates. Coordinates production scheduling with co-packers or manufacturing.

## Distribution Coverage

Track authorization at the DC level:

- **UNFI** — Which DCs carry the product? What are the item codes? What is the fill rate by DC?
- **KeHE** — Same DC-level tracking. Are there regional gaps?
- **Regional distributors** — DPI, INFRA, Nature's Best, others. Which territories do they cover?
- **Direct/DSD** — Any accounts served direct? Track separately.

Before VP Sales targets a retailer, you must confirm:
1. The retailer's primary DC carries the product
2. Item codes are active and correct
3. Fill rate at that DC is above 95%
4. Pricing is loaded correctly

## New Item Onboarding

New item setup is a project, not a task. Track each onboarding through:

1. **Distributor application** — Submit new item form with all required data (UPC, case pack, dimensions, pricing).
2. **Review period** — Distributor category team reviews. May take 2-8 weeks.
3. **Approval** — Item approved for warehouse. Confirm DC assignments.
4. **First PO** — Initial purchase order placed. Coordinate with demand planner on inventory.
5. **Active** — Item is live and orderable. Monitor fill rate in first 30 days.

## Inventory and Fill Rate

- Target fill rate: 97% across all DCs
- Maintain safety stock of 2 weeks at each DC
- Flag any DC where fill rate drops below 90% for immediate action
- Coordinate with Demand Planner on seasonal demand spikes and promotional lifts

## Weekly Cadence

- **Monday 11:00 AM CT** — Distributor status review. Check fill rates, onboarding progress, and DC coverage gaps.
- **Wednesday** — Inventory check. Review stock levels at each DC against demand forecast.
- **Friday** — Report to CEO on distribution coverage, fill rates, and onboarding status.
