# Account Review Template

Standard sections for a comprehensive retail account review. Use this template for QBRs, account planning sessions, and strategic reviews.

## Section 1: Account Overview

**Purpose**: Establish context for the review. Answer: "What is this account and where do we stand?"

Required fields:
- Account name and type (independent, regional chain, national chain)
- Relationship tenure (how long has this been an account?)
- Account status (active, dormant, new, at-risk)
- Primary point of contact (buyer name and title)
- Broker managing the relationship (if applicable)

Health indicators:
- **Active**: Meeting in last 30 days, open opportunities, products authorized
- **Engaged**: Open opportunities but no recent meeting (30-60 days)
- **Dormant**: No meetings or opportunity movement in 60+ days
- **At-risk**: Lost opportunity, buyer contact change, or distribution issue

## Section 2: Key Contacts

**Purpose**: Map the relationship landscape. Answer: "Who do we know and who do we need to know?"

For each contact:
- Name, title, role (buyer, category manager, merchandiser, etc.)
- Communication preference (email, phone, in-person)
- Last contact date and context
- Engagement level (active champion, neutral, resistant)
- Reporting relationship (who does this person report to?)

Gaps to identify:
- Is the primary buyer identified and engaged?
- Is there a category manager above/alongside the buyer?
- Are there store-level contacts for in-store execution?
- Is the broker contact current and active?
- Has there been buyer turnover? (New buyers = reset opportunity or risk)

## Section 3: Opportunity Pipeline

**Purpose**: Assess commercial momentum. Answer: "What's in the pipeline and is it moving?"

Current pipeline:
- List all open opportunities with stage, value, and days in stage
- Flag any stalled opportunities (per pipeline-review-framework.md thresholds)
- Note expected close dates and any hard deadlines (category reviews)

Historical pipeline:
- Win rate at this account (won / total closed)
- Average deal value
- Average cycle time (days from prospecting to won/lost)
- Recent wins and losses with reasons

Pipeline health assessment:
- Is the pipeline growing, stable, or shrinking?
- Are we moving opportunities forward or letting them stall?
- Is there whitespace (product families not yet pitched)?

## Section 4: Product Coverage

**Purpose**: Identify authorized products and whitespace. Answer: "What are they carrying and what should they be carrying?"

Coverage matrix:
- Product families authorized at this account
- Product families pitched but not yet authorized (and current stage)
- Product families not yet pitched (true whitespace)

For authorized products:
- Authorization date
- Current sellsheet status (current, outdated, missing)
- Any known performance data (velocity, turns — from SPINS when available)
- Promotional history at this account

For whitespace:
- Fit assessment: Would this product family fit the retailer's assortment?
- Competitive presence: Are competitor equivalents on shelf?
- Barrier assessment: Why hasn't this been pitched yet? (timing, fit, distribution)

## Section 5: Distribution Status

**Purpose**: Verify supply chain readiness. Answer: "Can we physically get product to this retailer's shelves?"

For each product family:
- Which distributor(s) serve this account?
- Which DC(s) supply stores for this account?
- Is the product authorized at those specific DCs?
- Are distributor codes active?

Common failure modes:
- Product authorized by buyer but not at serving DC
- DC authorization active but distributor code pending
- Product carried at some DCs but not the one serving this retailer's geography
- Distributor code recently deactivated (inventory issue, pricing issue)

## Section 6: Competitive Position

**Purpose**: Understand the competitive landscape. Answer: "Who else is on the shelf and how do we compare?"

Note: This section requires SPINS data or manual intelligence. Flag as incomplete if data is not available.

When data is available:
- Category dollar share at this retailer
- ACV distribution vs. competitors
- Price positioning (premium, parity, value)
- Recent competitive gains or losses
- Promotional activity comparison

When data is not available:
- Note any competitive intel from buyer conversations or store visits
- Flag this section for manual research before the review

## Section 7: Action Plan

**Purpose**: Define specific next steps. Answer: "What are we doing about all this?"

Action plan format:
- 3-5 specific, time-bound actions
- Each action has an owner and deadline
- Prioritized by impact and urgency

Common action categories:
1. **Sell-in actions**: New product pitches, category review submissions
2. **Relationship actions**: Schedule meetings, identify new contacts, re-engage dormant contacts
3. **Distribution actions**: Initiate DC authorization, resolve code issues
4. **Content actions**: Update sellsheets, prepare presentations
5. **Follow-up actions**: Close loops on previous meeting commitments
