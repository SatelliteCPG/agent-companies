# Buyer Meeting Checklist

Standard preparation checklist for retailer buyer meetings. Use this to ensure the meeting brief covers all required elements.

## Required Sections

### 1. Account History

- How long has this retailer been an account?
- What products do they currently carry?
- What is the overall relationship health? (active, dormant, new)
- Any known issues or escalations in the past 6 months?

### 2. Recent Opportunities

- List all open opportunities with current stage
- Highlight any that are stalled (no activity in 14+ days)
- Note expected close dates and any deadlines (category review windows)
- Calculate total pipeline value at this account

### 3. Competitive Context

**Note**: Satellite CRM does not currently hold competitive data. This section must be populated manually or from SPINS when available.

- Who else is in the category set at this retailer?
- What shelf space do competitors hold?
- Any recent competitive wins or losses?
- Price positioning vs. competitors

### 4. Talking Points

Generate 3-5 talking points based on CRM data:

- **Follow-up items** from last meeting (check meeting notes)
- **Pipeline advancement** — what stage should the next opportunity move to?
- **New products** — any launches the buyer hasn't seen yet?
- **Promotional opportunities** — upcoming ad windows or seasonal events
- **Distribution expansion** — new DCs that could serve this retailer

### 5. Product Highlights

For each product being discussed:
- Product name, pack size, UPC
- Current sellsheet (confirm it's current — check last updated date)
- Distributor authorization status at relevant DCs
- Suggested retail price and margin story
- Key differentiation claims

### 6. Samples Status

- Are samples needed for this meeting?
- Have samples been requested/shipped?
- What is the sample delivery timeline?
- Any special sample requirements (frozen, refrigerated, specific SKUs)?

## Pre-Meeting Day-Of Checklist

- [ ] Reviewed the brief within 1 hour of the meeting
- [ ] Sellsheets loaded on device or printed
- [ ] Samples packed and temperature-appropriate
- [ ] Buyer contact info confirmed (direct line, email)
- [ ] Meeting logistics confirmed (room, video link, address)
- [ ] Pricing sheet ready with current distributor costs
- [ ] Previous meeting recap reviewed for any open action items
- [ ] Competitive notes reviewed (if available)

## Post-Meeting Actions

After the meeting, run the `email-triage` skill on any follow-up emails, and manually update:
- Opportunity stages based on buyer feedback
- Contact records if new people were introduced
- Meeting record with outcomes and action items
- Sample requests if buyer asked for samples
