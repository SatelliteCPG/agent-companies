# Promotional Planning Guide

Reference document for planning and measuring CPG retail promotions. Covers promotion types, vendor funding mechanisms, lift measurement, and calendar management for natural/specialty retail.

## Promotion Types

### Temporary Price Reduction (TPR)
- **What**: Reduced retail price for a defined period (typically 1-4 weeks)
- **Execution**: Price change in POS system, shelf tag update, optional signage
- **Typical discount**: 10-25% off regular retail
- **Best for**: Volume driving, trial generation, competitive response
- **Lead time**: 4-6 weeks for pricing system entry and vendor coordination
- **Measurement**: Unit lift vs baseline, pantry loading check post-promo

### Endcap / Off-Shelf Display
- **What**: Product displayed outside regular shelf location (endcap, power wing, floor display)
- **Execution**: Display setup, product stocking, signage
- **Typical lift**: 200-500% vs in-aisle sales (highly variable)
- **Best for**: New items, seasonal, impulse purchase drivers
- **Lead time**: 6-8 weeks for display procurement and store scheduling
- **Measurement**: Display sales vs in-aisle sales during same period

### Circular / Ad Feature
- **What**: Product featured in the retailer's weekly ad circular (print or digital)
- **Execution**: Ad space allocation, photography, pricing, distribution
- **Typical lift**: 50-200% depending on page position and discount depth
- **Best for**: Traffic driving, brand awareness, seasonal pushes
- **Lead time**: 8-12 weeks for ad production schedules
- **Measurement**: Ad week sales vs non-ad weeks, new customer acquisition

### Sampling / Demo
- **What**: In-store product sampling with demo staff
- **Execution**: Demo staff scheduling, product allocation, store coordination
- **Typical conversion**: 5-15% of samplers make a purchase
- **Best for**: New items, premium items, products that benefit from taste/trial
- **Lead time**: 4-6 weeks for demo company booking and store approval
- **Measurement**: Same-day sales vs non-demo days, 4-week post-demo velocity

### BOGO / Multi-Buy
- **What**: Buy one get one free, buy 2 for $X, or similar multi-unit offers
- **Execution**: POS programming for multi-buy pricing
- **Typical lift**: 100-300% on unit volume
- **Best for**: Pantry loading, trial of multiple varieties, basket building
- **Lead time**: 4-6 weeks
- **Measurement**: Unit lift, average units per transaction, pantry loading effect

## Vendor Funding Mechanisms

### Manufacturer Chargeback (MCB)
- **How it works**: Retailer runs the promotion and invoices the vendor after the promotional period
- **Timing**: Claim submitted within 30-90 days of promo end (vendor-dependent)
- **Documentation**: Proof of performance (ad tear sheet, scan data, display photos)
- **Risk**: Claim denial if documentation is incomplete or promo deviated from agreement
- **Key tracking**: Submission deadlines, claim amounts, payment status

### Off-Invoice Deal
- **How it works**: Vendor reduces the invoice price for the promotional period
- **Timing**: Price reduction applies to orders placed during the deal period
- **Documentation**: Purchase orders showing deal pricing
- **Risk**: Over-ordering to capture deal pricing (forward buying)
- **Key tracking**: Deal start/end dates, qualifying order quantities

### Scan Deal / Performance-Based
- **How it works**: Vendor pays a per-unit rebate based on actual POS scans
- **Timing**: Payment typically monthly or quarterly based on scan data
- **Documentation**: POS scan data by store and period
- **Risk**: Requires accurate scan data capture; disputes over data accuracy
- **Key tracking**: Scan data submission, rebate calculation, payment receipt

### Slotting / New Item Fee
- **How it works**: One-time payment from vendor for shelf space or new item placement
- **Timing**: Payment before or at time of first shipment
- **Documentation**: Vendor agreement
- **Risk**: Vendor may view as sunk cost with no performance expectation
- **Key tracking**: Payment receipt, item performance at 90 days (justify the slot)

## Promotional Calendar Planning

### Annual Calendar Framework

| Quarter | Focus | Promotional Priorities |
|---------|-------|----------------------|
| Q1 (Jan-Mar) | New Year, health-focused | New items from holiday submissions, health/wellness promos |
| Q2 (Apr-Jun) | Spring, summer prep | Seasonal transitions, outdoor/grilling items, new spring items |
| Q3 (Jul-Sep) | Summer, back-to-school | Peak seasonal, back-to-school, Labor Day |
| Q4 (Oct-Dec) | Holiday, year-end | Thanksgiving, holiday gifting, year-end clearance |

### Planning Rules

1. **No unfunded promotions**: Every promotional slot requires confirmed vendor funding before go-live.
2. **Category balance**: No single vendor should dominate the promotional calendar for a category.
3. **Strategic alignment**: Promotions must align with category strategy (do not promote items you plan to discontinue).
4. **Measurement commitment**: Every promotion must have a pre-defined baseline and lift target.
5. **Post-promo review**: Every promotion gets a post-event analysis within 2 weeks of completion.

### Lift Benchmarks (Natural/Specialty Retail)

| Promo Type | Low Lift | Average Lift | High Lift |
|------------|----------|-------------|-----------|
| TPR (10-15% off) | 20% | 50% | 100% |
| TPR (20-25% off) | 50% | 100% | 200% |
| Endcap display | 100% | 300% | 500% |
| Circular feature | 30% | 100% | 200% |
| Circular + TPR | 75% | 150% | 300% |
| BOGO | 100% | 200% | 400% |
| Sampling/demo | 25% (day-of) | 50% (day-of) | 100% (day-of) |

**Note**: Lift benchmarks are approximate and vary significantly by category, brand, seasonality, and competitive context. Build retailer-specific benchmarks over time based on actual post-promo results.
