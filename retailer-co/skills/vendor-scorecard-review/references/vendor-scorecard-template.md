# Vendor Scorecard Template

Standard template and thresholds for evaluating vendor performance. Used by the Vendor Compliance Manager in weekly reviews and by category managers during category reviews.

## Scoring Framework

### Metric Definitions

#### Fill Rate
**Definition**: Percentage of ordered units that were actually shipped by the vendor.
**Formula**: (Units shipped / Units ordered) x 100
**Measurement period**: Rolling 4 weeks
**Data source**: Warehouse receiving logs vs purchase orders

| Level | Range | Action |
|-------|-------|--------|
| Green | 97%+ | No action — meets standard |
| Yellow | 95-97% | Vendor notification — 30-day improvement window |
| Red | <95% | Formal improvement plan — 14-day review |

**Common causes of low fill rate**:
- Production capacity constraints
- Raw material shortages
- Forecasting misalignment
- Seasonal demand spikes
- New item launch ramp-up issues

#### On-Time Delivery (OTIF)
**Definition**: Percentage of deliveries that arrived on or before the scheduled delivery date.
**Formula**: (On-time deliveries / Total deliveries) x 100
**Measurement period**: Rolling 4 weeks
**Data source**: Delivery timestamps vs PO expected delivery dates

| Level | Range | Action |
|-------|-------|--------|
| Green | 95%+ | No action — meets standard |
| Yellow | 90-95% | Vendor notification — 30-day improvement window |
| Red | <90% | Formal improvement plan — 14-day review |

**Common causes of late delivery**:
- Transportation issues (carrier delays, routing problems)
- Order processing delays at vendor
- DC appointment scheduling conflicts
- Weather or supply chain disruptions

#### Shelf-Life at Receipt
**Definition**: Percentage of remaining shelf life when product arrives at the DC.
**Formula**: (Remaining shelf life at receipt / Total shelf life) x 100
**Measurement period**: Rolling 4 weeks
**Data source**: Receiving inspection records

| Level | Range | Action |
|-------|-------|--------|
| Green | 75%+ remaining | No action — meets standard |
| Yellow | 60-75% remaining | Vendor notification — investigate production-to-ship timeline |
| Red | <60% remaining | Formal improvement plan — may indicate inventory management issues at vendor |

#### Quality Incidents
**Definition**: Number of quality events including recalls, consumer complaints traced to product, packaging defects, and foreign material.
**Measurement period**: Rolling quarter
**Data source**: Quality team incident reports

| Level | Count | Action |
|-------|-------|--------|
| Green | 0 per quarter | No action — meets standard |
| Yellow | 1 per quarter | Root cause analysis required from vendor within 10 business days |
| Red | 2+ per quarter | Formal improvement plan — vendor quality audit may be required |

#### Promotional Execution
**Definition**: Percentage of agreed promotional commitments that were executed correctly (right price, right timing, right product).
**Measurement period**: Per promotional event
**Data source**: Promo compliance checks vs promotional agreements

| Level | Range | Action |
|-------|-------|--------|
| Green | 95%+ | No action — meets standard |
| Yellow | 85-95% | Vendor notification — review execution process |
| Red | <85% | Formal improvement plan — may affect future promo eligibility |

## Vendor Tiers

Vendor tier determines priority for shelf space, endcap allocation, and promotional opportunities.

| Tier | Criteria | Benefits |
|------|----------|----------|
| Preferred | All metrics green for 4+ consecutive quarters | Priority shelf placement, first access to promotional slots, category captain consideration |
| Standard | No red metrics, max 1 yellow | Standard shelf allocation, standard promo access |
| Probation | Any red metric or 3+ yellow | Reduced shelf space risk, limited promo access, active improvement plan |
| New | First 90 days | Monitored closely, 90-day formal review |

## Improvement Plan Process

1. **Issue**: Vendor Compliance Manager identifies red or persistent yellow metrics.
2. **Notify**: Written notification to vendor with specific metrics, targets, and timeline.
3. **Root cause**: Vendor provides root cause analysis within 10 business days.
4. **Action plan**: Vendor submits improvement plan with milestones.
5. **Monitor**: Bi-weekly check-ins during improvement period.
6. **Resolve or escalate**: At end of improvement period, vendor either meets targets or is escalated to Director of Vendor Relations for tier review.
