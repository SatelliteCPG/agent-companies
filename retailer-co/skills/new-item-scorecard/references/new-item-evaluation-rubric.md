# New Item Evaluation Rubric

Standard evaluation criteria for scoring new product submissions from vendors and brokers. Used by the New Item Evaluator and category managers to make consistent, data-driven assortment decisions.

## Scoring Dimensions

### 1. Margin

Evaluate the product's margin contribution relative to category norms.

**Data needed**: Suggested retail price, wholesale/delivered cost, category average margin

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | Margin exceeds category average by 5%+ — strong financial contributor |
| 4 | Margin exceeds category average by 2-5% — above average contributor |
| 3 | Margin meets category average — financially neutral |
| 2 | Margin below average but above minimum threshold — acceptable if other scores are strong |
| 1 | Margin at minimum threshold — only acceptable for strategic items (traffic drivers, brand builders) |
| 0 | Margin below minimum threshold — does not meet financial requirements |

**Category margin benchmarks** (typical ranges, adjust per category):
- Grocery staples: 25-35%
- Natural/organic: 35-45%
- Specialty/premium: 40-55%
- Frozen: 30-40%
- Refrigerated: 35-45%

### 2. Category Fit

Evaluate whether the product addresses a real gap in the current assortment.

**Data needed**: Current shelf set, category strategy document, consumer insights

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | Fills a documented consumer need with no current offering — clear white space |
| 4 | Addresses an underserved segment where current offerings are limited |
| 3 | Adds meaningful variety to a segment with adequate but not excessive coverage |
| 2 | Overlaps with existing items but offers minor differentiation |
| 1 | Largely duplicates an existing item — would require discontinuation of a current item |
| 0 | Direct duplicate — no incremental value to the assortment |

**Questions to ask**:
- Does the category strategy call for expansion in this segment?
- Would adding this item require removing an existing item? If so, which one and why?
- Does this item serve a consumer segment not currently reached?
- Is there consumer demand data supporting a gap? (Surveys, social trends, competitor presence)

### 3. Differentiation

Evaluate what makes this product meaningfully different from the current shelf set.

**Data needed**: Competitive shelf set analysis, product attributes, brand story

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | First-to-market — genuinely novel formulation, format, or positioning |
| 4 | Strong differentiation — unique ingredients, process, certification, or brand story |
| 3 | Meaningful differentiation — better taste, cleaner label, compelling brand |
| 2 | Minor differentiation — different flavor or slightly different positioning |
| 1 | Packaging-level differentiation only — no functional or formulation difference |
| 0 | No meaningful differentiation from existing shelf set |

**Differentiation signals to evaluate**:
- Novel ingredients or formulation (functional, clean label, allergen-free)
- Unique format or packaging that improves consumer experience
- Strong brand story (founder story, mission-driven, certification-heavy)
- Consumer trend alignment (plant-based, gut health, low sugar, regenerative)
- Price point innovation (premium tier not currently served, or value tier missing)

### 4. Supplier Readiness

Evaluate the vendor's ability to reliably supply at scale.

**Data needed**: Vendor profile, fill rate history, DC authorization status, production capacity

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | Established vendor, 97%+ fill rate, national DC authorization, proven scale |
| 4 | Established vendor, 95%+ fill rate, regional DC authorization |
| 3 | Growing vendor, reliable track record, DC authorization in process |
| 2 | Early-stage vendor, limited DC authorization, some fulfillment history |
| 1 | Startup, no DC authorization, untested fulfillment capability |
| 0 | Pre-revenue or pre-production — cannot currently fulfill orders |

**Supplier readiness checklist**:
- [ ] Vendor has active account with relevant distributor (UNFI, KeHE, or regional)
- [ ] Item codes are set up or setup timeline is confirmed
- [ ] Production capacity can handle projected order volume
- [ ] Insurance and food safety certifications are current
- [ ] Vendor has dedicated point of contact for order issues

## Minimum Thresholds

- **Automatic decline**: Total score below 6 OR margin score of 0
- **Conditional acceptance**: Supplier readiness score of 0 or 1 requires a vendor improvement plan before shelf placement
- **Fast-track override**: VP Merchandising can fast-track a strategic item regardless of score (e.g., exclusive partnership, competitive response)

## Review Cadence

- New item evaluations are completed within 5 business days of submission
- Scored items are reviewed at the weekly new item review meeting (Tuesday 10 AM CT)
- Items waitlisted can be resubmitted after 90 days with documented improvements
