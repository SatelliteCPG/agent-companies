# Retailer Co

Agent company for running the merchandising operation of a CPG retailer like Whole Foods. A CPG retailer receives product submissions from brands and brokers, evaluates items against category strategy, manages vendor scorecards, plans promotional calendars, and optimizes shelf space for margin and velocity. That means running category reviews, scoring new item submissions (50-200 per category per quarter), tracking vendor compliance on fill rate and on-time delivery, planning TPRs and endcap features with 6-12 week lead times, and analyzing POS data to inform assortment decisions. This agent company covers all of it — category management, vendor relations, merchandising, promotions, and analytics — with 11 agents organized into the same functional teams a real retailer merchandising division runs.

## Operating Principles

### Category-First Decision Making

Every assortment, vendor, and promotional decision starts with category strategy. Define the category role (destination, routine, seasonal, convenience), set performance scorecards, and evaluate every item and vendor against that strategy. Category managers own P&L responsibility for their categories.

### Vendor Evaluation and Compliance

Vendors earn shelf space through performance, not relationships. Scorecards track fill rate, on-time delivery, shelf-life compliance, quality incidents, and promotional execution. New item submissions are scored on margin, category fit, differentiation, and supplier readiness. Vendors that underperform are put on improvement plans or replaced.

### Promotional Discipline

Every promotion — TPR, endcap, circular feature — has a planned cost, expected lift, and measurement window. Promotional calendars are planned 6-12 weeks out and coordinated with vendor funding commitments. Post-promotion analysis measures incremental lift vs baseline. Trade funds are allocated to categories and vendors that deliver measurable returns.

### Data-Driven Assortment

POS data, syndicated market data (SPINS/IRI/Nielsen), and vendor performance metrics drive all assortment decisions. Velocity per store per week, margin contribution, and basket affinity determine facings. Underperforming SKUs are flagged for discontinuation. Fast movers are expanded. Decisions are made on data, not vendor pitch decks.

### Structured Review Cadence

Category reviews follow the 8-step category management process: define, role, assess, scorecard, strategy, tactics, implement, review. Reviews happen 1-2 times per year per category with full vendor participation. Between reviews, weekly sales monitoring and monthly performance reports keep the team ahead of trends.
