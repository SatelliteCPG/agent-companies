# Multi-Brand Meeting Template

Standard template for broker buyer meetings covering multiple brands. Use this to ensure every multi-brand brief follows the same structure and covers all required elements.

## Required Sections

### 1. Meeting Header

Every brief must start with:
- Retailer name and buyer contact
- Meeting date, time, and type
- Count of brands being presented
- Meeting objective (new authorization, expand distribution, QBR, category review)

### 2. Account Snapshot

Summarize the retailer relationship at the portfolio level:
- How many brands does this retailer currently carry from our portfolio?
- What is the total pipeline value across all brands at this account?
- When was the last meeting and what was discussed?
- Any known issues or escalations in the past 6 months?

### 3. Brand-by-Brand Summary

For each brand being presented:
- **Status**: Existing (already carried) or Prospective (new pitch)
- **Product table**: Product name, pack size, price, sellsheet status, DC authorization
- **Talking point**: One sentence on what to lead with for this brand — based on pipeline stage, recent wins, or category trends
- **Ask**: What specific action do you want the buyer to take for this brand?

### 4. Cross-Brand Opportunities

This is the broker's unique value-add. Identify:
- **Category synergy**: Brands that fill different niches in the same category (price tiers, formats, consumer occasions)
- **White space**: Categories the retailer is underserved in where portfolio brands fit
- **Promotional bundles**: Brands that can run coordinated promotions (endcap displays, ad features)
- **Portfolio story**: The narrative that ties multiple brands together ("We bring you a complete natural snacks set")

### 5. Distribution Matrix

A single table showing DC authorization status for every product being discussed:
- Rows: Products (grouped by brand)
- Columns: Relevant DCs
- Cells: Authorized / Pending / Not Set Up

Highlight any product being pitched where the DC is not yet authorized — this is a blocker the broker must resolve before or during the meeting.

### 6. Competitive Context

**Note**: Satellite CRM does not currently hold competitive data. This section must be populated manually or from SPINS when available.

- Which competing brands are in the same category set at this retailer?
- What shelf space do competitors hold?
- Price positioning of portfolio brands vs competitors?
- Any recent competitive wins or losses?

### 7. Preparation Checklist

Pre-meeting day-of:
- [ ] Reviewed the brief within 1 hour of the meeting
- [ ] Sellsheets loaded for ALL brands (not just the primary pitch)
- [ ] Samples packed per brand (temperature-appropriate)
- [ ] Pricing confirmed with latest distributor costs for each brand
- [ ] Cross-brand positioning story rehearsed
- [ ] Buyer contact info confirmed
- [ ] Meeting logistics confirmed
- [ ] Previous meeting notes reviewed

### 8. Post-Meeting Actions

After every multi-brand meeting:
- Update opportunity stages for EVERY brand discussed (not just the one that got the most airtime)
- Log meeting record with outcomes per brand
- Send follow-up materials within 24 hours — per brand
- Create sample requests if buyer asked for samples from any brand
- Flag new void opportunities for Void Specialist
- Report meeting outcome to each relevant brand principal
