# Brand Evaluation Criteria

Detailed scoring rubric for each dimension of the brand evaluation scorecard.

## Dimension 1: Retailer Fit

How well do the brand's target retailers match the broker's existing retail relationships?

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | 90%+ of brand's target retailers are active broker accounts with strong buyer relationships |
| 4 | 70-90% overlap; most targets are active accounts |
| 3 | 50-70% overlap; some targets require new relationship building |
| 2 | 30-50% overlap; significant new account development required |
| 1 | 10-30% overlap; brand's market is mostly outside broker's network |
| 0 | No overlap; brand targets completely different retail channel |

**Key questions**:
- Which specific retailers does the brand want to be in?
- Do we have buyer relationships at those retailers?
- Have we placed products in similar categories at those retailers?
- Are there category review windows coming up at target retailers?

## Dimension 2: Distribution Readiness

Is the brand set up at distributors and ready to ship if a retailer says yes?

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | Active at UNFI and KeHE with codes at all major DCs; fill rates above 95% |
| 4 | Active at one major distributor with 70%+ DC coverage; fill rates above 90% |
| 3 | Set up at one distributor but coverage is regional; some DCs pending |
| 2 | Distributor relationship exists but codes not yet active; setup in progress |
| 1 | Has a distributor contact but no active setup; would need 4-8 weeks to activate |
| 0 | No distributor relationship; would need to initiate from scratch |

**Key questions**:
- Which distributors is the brand currently set up with?
- What is their fill rate history?
- Are there any active supply chain issues?
- How quickly could they scale production if we win a major retailer?

## Dimension 3: Portfolio Synergy

Does this brand complement or conflict with existing portfolio brands?

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | Fills a clear portfolio gap; no category overlap with existing clients; enhances cross-sell story |
| 4 | Complements existing brands; minimal overlap; different price point or format |
| 3 | Some category overlap but differentiated (e.g., organic vs conventional in same category) |
| 2 | Moderate overlap with existing client; may cause confusion with buyers |
| 1 | Significant overlap; would need to disclose to existing client; risk of conflict |
| 0 | Direct competitor to a top-performing existing client; taking this brand would risk losing the other |

**Key questions**:
- Which existing brands are in the same category?
- Would a buyer see these as complementary or competing?
- Does the existing client have a non-compete clause?
- Could we pitch both brands to the same buyer without conflict?

## Dimension 4: Margin Structure

Does the commission and revenue potential justify the rep time and overhead?

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | Commission above 7%; estimated Year 1 revenue justifies dedicated rep time |
| 4 | Commission 5-7%; revenue covers cost of service with margin |
| 3 | Commission 3-5%; revenue covers cost of service at breakeven |
| 2 | Commission 3-5% but limited retailer targets reduce revenue potential |
| 1 | Commission below 3%; would need high volume to justify |
| 0 | Commission structure is unclear or below minimum threshold |

**Key questions**:
- What is the proposed commission rate?
- What is the average order size per retailer?
- How many target retailers are realistic in Year 1?
- Is there a minimum guarantee or retainer?
- Does the brand pay for samples, trade show fees, or are those broker costs?

## Dimension 5: Category Demand

Is there market demand for this brand's products at retail?

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | Category growing 10%+; retailers actively seeking new brands; clear consumer trend |
| 4 | Category growing 5-10%; moderate retailer interest; established consumer demand |
| 3 | Category stable; replacement opportunities exist; brand has differentiation |
| 2 | Category flat or slightly declining; limited shelf space for new entrants |
| 1 | Category declining; retailers reducing shelf space; brand lacks differentiation |
| 0 | Category in significant decline; no retailer demand; brand has no clear positioning |

**Key questions**:
- What do SPINS/IRI data show for this category?
- Are retailers expanding or contracting shelf space in this category?
- What consumer trends support this product?
- How does the brand's pricing compare to category leaders?

## Overall Scoring

Calculate the unweighted average of all five dimensions. The Managing Partner may apply weighting based on strategic priorities:

**Suggested weights when strategic priorities differ**:
- If the broker needs to grow in a specific category: Weight Category Demand at 2x
- If the broker needs margin improvement: Weight Margin Structure at 2x
- If portfolio diversification is the priority: Weight Portfolio Synergy at 2x

Default: All dimensions weighted equally.
