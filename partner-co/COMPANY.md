# Partner Co

Agent company for running a CPG broker/partner operation like Greenseed. A CPG broker represents 50-100 brands simultaneously to retail buyers and distributors. That means managing a multi-brand sales pipeline across dozens of retailers, prepping buyer meetings that cover 3-10 brands in a single session, tracking void closures where authorized items aren't reaching store shelves, recovering invalid deductions on behalf of brand principals, coordinating category review submissions across brands and retailers, onboarding new brand clients, and reporting monthly performance back to each brand founder. This agent company covers all of it — sales, client services, operations, and analytics — with 12 agents organized into the same functional teams a real broker runs.

## Operating Principles

### Multi-Brand Pipeline Management

Every opportunity is tracked by brand and retailer. Pipeline views aggregate across the full brand portfolio — grouped by brand, by retailer, or by stage. No single-brand tunnel vision. Broker reps manage 50+ brand pipelines simultaneously, and the system reflects that reality.

### Buyer Meeting Prep (Multi-Brand)

A broker walks into a retailer meeting representing multiple brands. Meeting prep aggregates sell sheets, pricing, promo calendars, and distribution status across all brands relevant to that buyer. One meeting, many brands, one unified brief.

### Void Closure

Distribution is only half the battle. Authorized items that aren't stocked at store level are revenue left on the table. Void closure compares authorized items against distributor DC stocking data to surface gaps, then tracks closure actions by retailer and brand.

### Deduction Recovery

Retailers take deductions from brand payments — damaged goods, promo chargebacks, compliance fines. Brokers file disputes on behalf of brand clients, tracking amounts, deadlines, and resolution status. Every dollar recovered goes to the bottom line.

### Brand Principal Reporting

Brokers are accountable to the brands they represent. Monthly performance reports cover pipeline activity, new placements, lost authorizations, promo results, and deduction recovery — delivered to each brand founder or VP Sales.
